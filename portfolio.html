Selected Works

This portfolio presents a curated archive of historical documentary photographs focused on labor, inequality, migration, and living conditions. Click any image to enlarge it.

Shucking Oysters in a Canning Factory
c. 1911
Photographer: Lewis Hine
Medium: Photograph
Dimensions: Original archival photograph

This image documents children and women working in a seafood processing factory. It highlights how labor systems relied on vulnerable populations and reflects the economic pressures that shaped working-class life.

Source: Library of Congress

Coal Breaker Boys, South Pittston, Pennsylvania
1911
Photographer: Lewis Hine
Medium: Photograph
Dimensions: Original archival photograph

This photograph shows boys working in industrial coal operations, demonstrating how child labor was normalized in dangerous environments during industrial growth.

Source: Library of Congress

Mill Village Housing, North Carolina
c. 1908–1910
Photographer: Lewis Hine
Medium: Photograph
Dimensions: Original archival photograph

This image focuses on the living conditions surrounding industrial labor. It reflects how work environments extended into housing and shaped entire communities.

Source: Library of Congress

Mill Village and Factory, North Carolina
c. 1908–1910
Photographer: Lewis Hine
Medium: Photograph
Dimensions: Original archival photograph

This photograph shows the relationship between industrial production and residential space, highlighting how economic systems shaped both labor and daily life.

Source: Library of Congress

Newsboys Selling Newspapers, St. Louis, Missouri
1910
Photographer: Lewis Hine
Medium: Photograph
Dimensions: Original archival photograph

This image documents child labor in public spaces, showing how economic necessity extended beyond factories into everyday urban environments.

Source: Library of Congress

Full-Rigged Sailing Ship
c. 1900–1910
Medium: Photograph
Dimensions: Original archival photograph

This image reflects broader themes of movement and economic expansion. While not directly tied to labor conditions, it connects to systems of trade and migration.

Ellis Island Grounds and Buildings, New York

Medium: Photograph
Dimensions: Original archival photograph
